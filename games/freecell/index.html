How to Play FreeCell

Goal

Move all 52 cards onto the four foundations, one pile per suit, built up from Ace to King.

Setup

All 52 cards are dealt face-up into 8 tableau columns (the first four columns get 7 cards, the last four get 6). There is no hidden information - every card is visible from the start. Above the tableau are 4 empty free cells and 4 empty foundations.

Moving Cards

Click a card to select it, then click where you want it to go. Clicking a card selects the longest valid run starting there - a run is a sequence of cards descending in rank with alternating colors (red on black, black on red). Clicking the selected card again deselects it.

A tableau column accepts a run if it's empty, or if its top card is one rank higher than the bottom card of the run and the opposite color. Any single card can go into an empty free cell. A single card can go onto its foundation if it's the next rank that foundation needs. Cards sitting in a free cell can be played to the tableau or a foundation just like a tableau card.

Supermoves

You can move more than one card at a time as a "supermove," which the game simulates as if you moved each card through your empty free cells and empty columns one at a time. The more free cells and empty columns you have open, the more cards you can move at once: capacity is (1 + free cells open) × 2empty columns open. If you try to move a run bigger than your current capacity allows, the move is rejected and the game tells you why.

Winning

Get all 52 cards onto the foundations. There's no way to "lose" FreeCell outright - if you get stuck, head back to the Main Menu and deal a fresh game.
