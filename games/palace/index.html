How to Play Palace

Goal

Be the first to get rid of every card - your hand, your face-up pile, and your face-down pile. Whoever empties all three first wins immediately.

Setup

Each player gets 3 cards face-down, 3 face-up on top of those, and 3 in hand. Before play begins, you can swap cards between your hand and face-up pile - keep your strongest cards in hand, since face-up cards are visible to everyone and get played later, once your hand and the draw pile are gone.

Your Turn

Play one or more cards of the same rank from your hand onto the pile - each card must match or beat the rank of the top card (or satisfy an active restriction - see Special Cards). If you have no legal card, you must pick up the entire pile into your hand. After a legal play, draw back up to 3 cards from the draw pile if any remain.

Special Cards

  • 2 - always playable, resets the pile so the next player can play anything.
  • 7 - the next card played must be a 7 or lower (2s, 10s, and Jokers are still always OK).
  • 8 - skips the next player. Playing multiple 8s at once skips that many players.
  • 10 - always playable, burns the pile out of the game entirely, and you go again.
  • Joker - always playable, resets the pile like a 2, and reverses the direction of play.
  • Four cards of the same rank in a row also burns the pile, just like a 10.

Once Your Hand Runs Out

Once your hand and the draw pile are both empty, you play from your face-up cards. Once those are gone too, you play your face-down cards blind - flip one and hope it's legal. If it isn't, you pick up the whole pile plus that card.

Winning

The moment a player clears hand, face-up, and face-down all the way, the game ends and they win. Everyone else is ranked by how many cards they still hold.
